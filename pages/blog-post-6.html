🌊 Vitamin Sea

August 2023

Vitamin Sea… it's officially beach season!! & what comes to mind when you hear the word "SEA"? We immediately think: Water, Sand & of course the Sun — & yes, sunburns too.

Let's focus on The SUN — wait a sec, what does that have to do with nutrition?! (I'm getting to it… keep reading to find out 😉)

The Sun is nature's original source of Vitamin D. It uplifts us, gives us energy, provides warmth, & does so much more. Ok, if it's really that good for us — why does it cause harm? Because just like with any other thing, if you go overboard it turns into a curse rather than a blessing. The "Sun" is no different.

But what if there's something you can do to protect yourself from sunburns & sun damage other than just putting on SPF? Yep — you guessed it — that's where the nutrition aspect comes in!

Your SPF from Within

One way to protect your skin from sun damage or harmful UV rays is to make sure you are well supplied with Vitamin C (that's C not Sea 😉) — not just in your skincare, but through food as well. In fact, food should actually be your first source of protection!

Vitamin C is an antioxidant mainly known for its cell renewal properties, which helps with skin regeneration — making it crucial during the summer season.

You'll also need the help of Vitamin E (also an antioxidant) to combat the damage caused by the sun. And don't forget healthy fats (specifically Omega 3 & 9) — they provide the skin with certain needed nutrients to keep its elasticity & shape intact.

Think of this as your SPF from within — then go ahead and apply a layer of sunscreen as your outer layer of protection. This way you're not just outwardly taking care of your skin but you're giving it the nourishment it needs from within.

Try to get 15–30 minutes of sun exposure daily — either early in the morning or towards the end of the day when UV rays are low (usually before 10 AM or after 4 PM).

Food Sources to Prioritize This Summer

  • Vitamin C: Kiwi, strawberry, blueberries, cherries, pomegranate, citrus fruits, leafy greens, beets, matcha
  • Vitamin E: Almonds, hazelnuts, sunflower seeds, pine nuts, avocado, salmon, mango, red pepper, asparagus, broccoli
  • Healthy Fats: Avocado, salmon, sardines, eggs, olives, dairy products
  • Hydration: Drink water consistently throughout the day — you can also eat your water (cucumber, watermelon!)

A Note on Vitamin D Supplements

If you are currently taking a Vitamin D supplement, don't stop taking it just because summer is here. If you were deficient prior — or had a deficiency — you will most likely become deficient again when summer is over (if not before). Now that you're well SPFed from the inside as well as the outside — you're ready to dive into all the summer fun! ☀️
