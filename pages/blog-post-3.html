⏳🕰 Making Time for it!

December 2022

Regardless what your "it" might be, there's one thing you should know:

It Requires Time!!

But wait — it's a bit more complicated than that, and in my opinion the most important part is the time you schedule out to prep and plan for whatever it is you want to do.

The 3-Phase Process

  1. Time to plan out
  2. Time to prep & get ready
  3. Time to actually get it done

All of these components are part of the effort you put into a certain thing — and not only that, they also motivate you and prep your mind, easing you into the process, which will lead to a much more successful outcome.

Your "it" might be improving your overall health, dieting, or starting to exercise — but without you making time or putting in the effort, all it will ever be is only a "wish" that needs magic to turn into a reality!

What This Looks Like in Practice

For anything to be accomplished in life it needs work, and work requires time and dedication. For example:

  • If you want to eat better, you must put in time to shop for healthier options, go through what you already have and make a judgement call on what to keep and what to eliminate, and gather info on what food items you should get instead
  • If you struggle with having healthy meals ready during your work week, you need to set time aside to meal prep and cook for the week ahead
  • If your "it" is exercise, you know that you must plan before you dive straight into working out: Do I have workout clothes? Will I go to a gym? Do I need any equipment? What time of day and how many days per week? How will I track my results?

Surprised?! This is why planning is crucial — it makes you think about every aspect. Before your first workout, you need to set aside time to sit down and answer these questions & efficiently plan the routine that suits you.

The Real Question to Ask Yourself

The process is the same for anything you're embarking on — learning a new language, taking a new course, starting a business. All of these have one factor in common: "making time for it" in your schedule. And that includes both time for planning AND time for execution.

If you find yourself discouraged because you couldn't start on the right foot — before you jump & blame the diet or the exercise or claim they don't work — STOP & assess. Ask yourself:

  • "Did I put in the time to plan & organize?"
  • "Did I stick to the routine I chose and how consistent was I really?"

To make a wish come true it needs both time and a plan in order to turn into a reality — think of it like a recipe: you need to get the right ingredients, use the correct measurements, and follow the instructions so that the final product tastes good. Making time and planning efficiently are the only ingredients needed for the recipe of success!

Your New Year Resolution

Use this upcoming New Year as an opportunity to start right and change your habits. Make a resolution and follow it through — put in the time needed to achieve your goals, take full responsibility for your shortcomings, and try to fix them as you go.

Because we all know life happens and comes in the way — but if we always take time out to plan and reorganize, we will get back on track. All we need to do is block out an hour or two a week to come up with a new plan that works with our new schedule or lifestyle.

Remember — it's not only about being consistent but in equal parts your willingness to restart as many times as it takes, rather than giving up on yourself and calling it a day. Be Flexible but stay Focused!
